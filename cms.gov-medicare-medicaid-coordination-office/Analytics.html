CMS Data Tools
The MMCO has developed special tools to aid researchers and States in studying the care experience of people who are enrolled in both Medicare and Medicaid. These tools include the following:

  1. Medicare-Medicaid Linked Enrollee Analytic Data Source, version 2.0 Public Use File (MMLEADS-v2.0-PUF) - This public use file is an Excel document that makes the data within the CCW-hosted MMLEADS-v2.0 accessible to everyone, irrespective of technical readiness to analyze the files. The MMLEADS-v2.0-PUF contains summary information for full and partial benefit Medicare-Medicaid enrollees as well as Medicare-only and Medicaid-only with disability. Information on eligibility, enrollment, cost, use, and select health and mental health conditions is available for all states and the nation as a whole for years 2006 through 2010.
  2. Medicare-Medicaid Linked Enrollee Analytic Data Source, version 2.0 (MMLEADS-v2.0) – MMLEADS is a suite of linked Medicare and Medicaid claims’ summary files. MMLEADS allows for the examination of all Medicare and Medicaid enrollment and claims data for those who were dually enrolled in both programs. The summary files are primarily designed to address eligibility, enrollment, cost, use, and select health and mental health conditions. Available years include 2006 through 2010. More information on MMLEADS is available at https://www.ccwdata.org/web/guest/home
  3. Chronic Condition Data Warehouse (CCW) Condition Flags– The Chronic Condition Data Warehouse (CCW) is CMS’ database that contains multiple chronic condition indicators (i.e., "flags") on the Medicare, Medicaid and Medicare-Medicaid dually enrolled populations. All flags are available for calendar years 2000 through 2010, with Medicare-only flags also being available for more recent data. These flags were built from Medicare and Medicaid claims data across the following four separate sets:
    • Set 1. 27 Original CCW condition flags: This first set of flags was originally developed in order to facilitate research on chronic illness in the Medicare population and ultimately improve the quality of care and reduce program spending. These flags have since been extended to Medicaid-only beneficiaries, and the Medicare-Medicaid dual enrollees' flags were expanded to include their Medicaid experience.
    • Set 2. Mental Health and Tobacco-related Conditions: Anxiety Disorders; Conduct Disorders and Hyperkinetic Syndrome; Bipolar Disorder; Depressive Disorders; Personality Disorders; Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder; Schizophrenia; Schizophrenia and Other Psychotic Disorders; and Tobacco Use.
    • Set 3. Conditions Often Associated with Intellectual, Developmental and Physical Disability: Autism Spectrum Disorder; Cerebral Palsy; Cystic Fibrosis and Other Metabolic Developmental Disorders; Epilepsy; Intellectual Disabilities and Related Conditions; Learning Disabilities; Mobility Impairments; Multiple Sclerosis and Transverse Myelitis; Muscular Dystrophy; Other Developmental Delays; Sensory - Deafness and Hearing Impairment; Sensory - Blindness and Visual Impairment; Spina Bifida and Other Congenital Anomalies of the Nervous System; Spinal Cord Injury; Traumatic Brain Injury and Nonpsychotic Mental Disorders due to Brain Damage.
    • Set 4: Other Chronic Physical and Behavioral Health Conditions: Fibromyalgia, Chronic Pain and Chronic Fatigue; HIV/AIDS*; Leukemia and Lymphoma; Liver Disease, Cirrhosis, and Other Liver Conditions (excluding Hepatitis); Viral Hepatitis; Migraine and Other Chronic Headache; Obesity; Pressure Ulcers and Chronic Ulcer; and Peripheral Vascular Disease.
    • Six Additional HIV/AIDS-related flags include: HIV Screening; Antiretroviral Medication Use; HIV/AIDS Health Care Services Contact; HIV/AIDS program participation; SSA Disability-Qualifying Primary and Secondary Diagnoses.

Please visit https://www.ccwdata.org/web/guest/home for more information on MMLEADS and the CCW flags.
